First year

June 10, 2020
My first year at the University of Birmingham has come to an end, and although it was short lived; thank you corona, I can’t emphasise how quickly it has gone! It only seems like yesterday I was arriving at the university with a car full of new and clean IKEA kitchen ware. Trust me, I went a bit overboard! It’s equally as scary thinking I’ll be a second year student next year, where the work ramps up exponentially. Freshers was amazing. I met loads of new people and spent the whole time socialising with everyone. This was the perfect opportunity to join new societies as well, so I joined the go-karting team, the ski-team and also one of the university’s campus football teams. A lot of sport! This was also the first time where I had lived away from home, so the challenge of adulthood really stepped in quickly.
yeah yeah

First terms work

Social side aside, I’ve loved the topics I had this year in Computer science. We started the term learning Java script, and although this was a challenge for me at the start; as I had not done computing A-level, after a few weeks I got to grip of it and excelled through the tasks. I learnt a lot during this module, such as the fundamental lesson that planning before starting a project is always the way to go. Collaboration really helped me as well, as in some tasks we got given partially competed code, where we had to expand off the different methods. /p> Alongside the java aspect of the course, we also had Mathematics. This module focused on the mathematics behind computer science, covering a range of topics from regular languages to the complexity of NP and P problems. I had to think quite hard in this module, reading around the course to get a full grasp about the power these mathematic equations had. Due to being on a Bachelor of Science degree with Vodafone, I got given finance and business management as my widening horizons modules. Their goal was to give me a further insight into how organisations are structured and how they work their balance sheet. It really helped having past experience in Economics for this module, as I could implement everything I learnt for a level into my end of year dissertation.

second terms work

The second term consisted of Robotics, Artificial intelligence and Logic & computation. I loved Robotics because we could see our code running on a machine, instead of just a computer. Hardware vs software was a problem for the robots, as we would code a piece of software that would complete every task when tested in a software sandbox; however in a real life environment would fail. This involved a lot of trial and error but I toughly enjoyed this process. I found Artificial intelligence the hardest topic in computer science, as I was introduced to a lot of new alien terms. It took a lot of time to fully understand the ideas around machine learning, and all of the equations behind it. Logic and computation took a lot of time to fully understand but as the name suggests, when fully understood, it is quite logical. Every week got harder for this topic and understanding the base ideas was very important for this topic.
